# OctoAcme — Meeting Templates & Cadence Guide

## Purpose
Standardise recurring meeting formats to reduce preparation overhead, keep ceremonies purposeful, and ensure all relevant roles are represented and informed.

---

## Recommended Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Facilitator | Core Attendees |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Daily Standup | Daily (weekdays) | 15 min | Scrum Master | Dev, QA, UX, PM; Tech Writer (optional — attend when actively working on in-sprint docs) |
| Sprint Planning | Start of each sprint | 1–2 hrs | Scrum Master | PdM, Dev, QA, UX, PM |
| Sprint Review / Demo | End of each sprint | 30–60 min | PM or Scrum Master | All roles + Stakeholders |
| Sprint Retrospective | End of each sprint | 45–75 min | Scrum Master | Dev, QA, UX, PM, PdM |
| Weekly PM + PdM Sync | Weekly | 30 min | PM | PM, PdM |
| Backlog Refinement | Mid-sprint (1–2× per sprint) | 45–60 min | PdM / Scrum Master | PdM, Dev, QA, UX |
| Monthly Stakeholder Update | Monthly | 30–45 min | PM | PM, PdM, Stakeholders, Eng Manager |
| Risk Review | Weekly (embedded in PM sync) | 15 min | PM | PM, PdM, Eng Manager |

---

## Meeting Agenda Templates

### 1. Daily Standup (15 min)

**Purpose:** Surface progress, blockers, and dependencies so the team stays aligned.

**Agenda:**
1. Each attendee answers (1–2 min each):
   - What did I complete since the last standup?
   - What am I working on today?
   - Do I have any blockers or dependencies?
2. Scrum Master notes blockers for follow-up (offline, after standup).
3. Quick reminder of sprint goal and days remaining.

**Notes:**
- Keep to time — park detailed discussions for after the standup.
- Update the project board before or immediately after standup.

---

### 2. Sprint Planning (1–2 hrs)

**Purpose:** Select and commit to sprint work; ensure every item meets the Definition of Ready.

**Agenda:**
1. Sprint goal statement — PdM presents (10 min)
2. Capacity check — Scrum Master reviews team availability (5 min)
3. Backlog walkthrough — PdM walks top items; team asks clarifying questions (30–60 min)
4. Team commits to sprint backlog — Dev, QA, UX confirm estimates and acceptance criteria (20–30 min)
5. Assign owners and update project board (10 min)
6. Confirm Definition of Done is understood by all (5 min)

**Definition of Ready check (per item):**
- [ ] User story / ticket is clearly written with acceptance criteria
- [ ] Dependencies identified and unblocked
- [ ] Design assets available (if UI work)
- [ ] Estimated (story points or T-shirt size)
- [ ] Reviewed in backlog refinement

---

### 3. Sprint Review / Demo (30–60 min)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate completed work, gather stakeholder feedback, and validate against sprint goal.

**Agenda:**
1. Sprint goal recap — PM/Scrum Master (3 min)
2. Demo of completed features — Dev, UX, QA rotate through demos (20–40 min)
3. Stakeholder feedback and questions (10 min)
4. Metrics check — velocity, acceptance rate, quality (5 min)
5. Next sprint preview — PdM shares top backlog items (5 min)

**Notes:**
- Only demo work that meets the Definition of Done.
- Capture feedback as backlog items immediately.

---

### 4. Sprint Retrospective (45–75 min)

**Purpose:** Inspect team process and create a short list of actionable improvements.

**Agenda:**
1. Set the stage — Scrum Master ensures psychological safety (5 min)
2. What went well? — silent brainstorm, then share (10–15 min)
3. What could be improved? — silent brainstorm, then share (10–15 min)
4. Follow-up on previous action items (5 min)
5. Dot vote on top improvement themes (5 min)
6. Define 2–3 action items with owners and due dates (10–15 min)
7. Close and thank the team (2 min)

**Output:** Action items captured as backlog items or tracked in the retro doc.

---

### 5. Weekly PM + PdM Sync (30 min)

**Purpose:** Maintain alignment on delivery status, risks, roadmap priorities, and stakeholder needs.

**Agenda:**
1. Progress vs. plan — PM reviews milestone status (5 min)
2. Risk register review — PM walks open risks; PdM flags product risks (10 min)
3. Stakeholder update — discuss any stakeholder concerns or incoming requests (5 min)
4. Roadmap alignment — PdM previews upcoming backlog items needing PM coordination (5 min)
5. Actions and decisions (5 min)

**Standing agenda items:**
- Any blockers requiring escalation?
- Documentation or release notes due?
- Any upcoming milestones in the next two weeks?

---

### 6. Backlog Refinement (45–60 min)

**Purpose:** Ensure the top items in the backlog meet the Definition of Ready before they enter a sprint.

**Agenda:**
1. PdM presents next priority items with context (15 min)
2. Team clarifies requirements, edge cases, and acceptance criteria (20 min)
3. Estimation (story points or T-shirt sizes) (10 min)
4. Flag design or technical dependencies (10 min)
5. Update tickets and confirm readiness (5 min)

**Definition of Ready reminder:**
- User story is written from the user's perspective
- Acceptance criteria are testable
- Design assets linked (if applicable)
- Dependencies noted and flagged

---

### 7. Monthly Stakeholder Update (30–45 min)

**Purpose:** Keep leadership and cross-functional stakeholders informed of project health, roadmap, and decisions needed.

**Agenda:**
1. Executive summary — PM (5 min)
2. Milestone and delivery status (10 min)
3. Key risks and mitigations (5 min)
4. Roadmap highlights for the next 30–60 days (5–10 min)
5. Decisions / asks from stakeholders (10 min)

**Supporting materials:**
- Status dashboard or slide deck prepared by PM
- Updated risk register reviewed by PM + PdM before meeting

---

## Meeting Hygiene Guidelines
- **Record decisions** in meeting notes and share within 24 hours.
- **Assign actions** with a single owner and a clear due date.
- **Start and end on time** to respect everyone's calendar.
- **Include remote participants** — use video and shared whiteboards.
- **Pre-read materials** shared at least 24 hours before planning or stakeholder meetings.
